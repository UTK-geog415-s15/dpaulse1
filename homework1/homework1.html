  • Sex ratio at birth is defined on the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) website as the number of boys born alive for every 100 girls that are born alive.

  2. 21st century US: Today, the sex ratio may still have more males, but the rate is probably at (or at least much closer) to its natural rate. Thanks to modern medicine, the infant mortality rate has declined and both genders are seen as equally important.

This policy led to Chinas sex ratio at birth to reach as high as 117:100 (the natural rate is usually between 103:100 and 107:100).

Question 6

The purpose of this question is to help you understand the variance.

The formula for sample variance \(\sigma^2\) of a dataset is: \[\frac{\sum_{i=1}^N (x_i - \bar{x})^2}{N-1}\] Describe in words what each part of this equation is, and using the concept of distance describe what the sample variance measures.

  • Numerator: \({\sum_{1=1}^N (x_i - \bar{x})^2}\)

This part of the equation sums up the squared differences between each x value and the mean.

  • Denominator: \({N-1}\)

Basically, the denominator serves to make the value above into an average by dividing the numerator by (roughly) the number of observations in the data.

  • The sample variance is basically the average distance from the mean squared. Squaring the value is done to eliminate the cancelling out of positive and negative differences. If this step was not performed, we would likely obtain a value very close to zero (assuming the data is approximately Normal).
